# Privacy Policy

Last updated: `May 8, 2026`

## 1. Overview
This Privacy Policy explains how Overclock.lol ("Overclock," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use our platform.

Overclock is an Overwatch-focused LFG and social platform. If you use Overclock, you agree that we may process your information as described in this Privacy Policy.

## 2. Information We Collect
We collect information from a few different sources.

### Information you provide or publish
- profile information such as display name, username, bio, rank, heroes, region, and related profile fields
- public posts, LFG content, and other content you choose to publish
- uploaded media such as profile images and cover images
- messages or reports you send to us, if applicable

### Information from Discord OAuth
When you sign in with Discord, we may receive limited account information from Discord, such as:
- your Discord account identifier
- username or display information made available through the OAuth flow
- avatar or related profile metadata made available through the OAuth flow
- authentication/session information needed to complete sign-in

We only receive the data made available through the permissions and account flow we use.

### Information collected automatically
- IP address and approximate network/device information
- browser and device information
- page visits, navigation events, and basic usage events
- timestamps and request metadata
- error logs, security logs, and abuse-prevention signals
- rate-limiting and anti-abuse records

## 3. How We Use Information
We use information to:
- create and maintain your account
- authenticate users through Discord
- display public profiles and public posts
- provide LFG, connection, and community features
- host and serve uploaded media
- moderate content and enforce platform rules
- investigate abuse, fraud, spam, harassment, security incidents, or rule violations
- investigate reports, predatory behavior concerns, threats, exploitation, impersonation, or safety risks involving minors or other users
- operate, improve, debug, and maintain the platform
- measure performance, reliability, and basic product usage
- comply with legal obligations and protect our rights

## 4. Public Profile and Content Visibility
- Overclock is designed around public profile and community discovery features.
- Information you publish may be visible to other users and may also be visible publicly on profile or listing pages.
- Public information may include usernames, display names, bios, rank information, preferred heroes, profile images, cover images, and LFG content.
- Please do not publish sensitive information you do not want others to see.
- Because Overclock is a public-facing community platform, content you choose to share may be copied, reshared, cached, indexed, or retained by other users, third-party services, or internet systems outside our control.

## 5. Uploaded Media
- If you upload profile or cover media, we store that media using our infrastructure and storage providers, including storage services used through Supabase or similar providers.
- Uploaded media may be processed, cached, resized, or delivered through third-party infrastructure as needed to run the service.
- If you replace or delete media, we may remove old files immediately or during later cleanup and retention workflows.
- Old media may remain temporarily in backups, caches, or queued cleanup processes.

## 6. Cookies, Sessions, and Authentication
- We use cookies, tokens, or similar session mechanisms to keep users signed in and to support authentication flows.
- These technologies may be used for login state, security, fraud prevention, session continuity, and basic platform functionality.
- If you disable essential authentication or session technologies, parts of Overclock may not work properly.

## 7. Analytics, Logging, and Rate Limiting
- We may use analytics, diagnostics, and logging tools to understand platform usage, measure reliability, prevent abuse, and improve the product.
- We may use rate limiting, request tracking, and related systems to protect the platform from spam, scraping, attacks, or misuse.
- These systems may process account identifiers, request metadata, timestamps, IP addresses, and related activity data.
- Some logs and abuse-prevention records may be retained for a limited period beyond normal content deletion where reasonably necessary for security, moderation, investigations, platform integrity, or legal compliance.

## 8. Third-Party Providers
We rely on third-party services to operate Overclock. Depending on the feature, those providers may process information on our behalf or as independent services.

These may include:
- Discord for authentication
- Supabase for database, authentication, storage, and related infrastructure
- hosting, CDN, image delivery, logging, analytics, monitoring, and security providers

Your use of third-party services may also be subject to those providers' own terms and privacy policies.

Information may be processed across these providers as needed to authenticate users, store media, deliver public pages, monitor reliability, prevent abuse, investigate reports, and maintain the platform.

## 9. How We Share Information
We may share information:
- with service providers and infrastructure vendors that help us run Overclock
- when content is made public through your profile or posts
- with moderators, admins, contractors, or operators who need access for support, safety, moderation, abuse prevention, or operations
- to comply with law, legal process, or valid government requests
- to protect users, the public, or our rights, property, and safety
- in connection with a merger, financing, acquisition, asset sale, or similar transaction

We do not promise that information will stay private if you intentionally publish it publicly on the platform.

## 10. Moderation and Safety Use
We may review and use account, profile, post, media, log, and report data to:
- investigate abuse or platform misuse
- enforce our Terms and community rules
- remove harmful content
- respond to safety concerns
- prevent evasion of moderation or platform restrictions

This may include reviewing:
- user reports and related context
- public profile and post content
- uploaded media
- account activity and linked account signals
- logs, request metadata, rate-limit events, and abuse-prevention signals
- content or records relevant to threats, harassment, predatory behavior, exploitation, impersonation, fraud, or safety concerns

## 11. Retention
- We keep information for as long as reasonably necessary to operate the platform, provide features, enforce rules, resolve disputes, comply with law, and protect the service.
- Different categories of data may be kept for different periods.
- Replaced or deleted profile content and media may be removed immediately, on a delay, or as part of periodic cleanup.
- Some data may remain in logs, backups, audit trails, or abuse-prevention systems for a limited or longer period where reasonably necessary.
- We may preserve specific records for longer where reasonably necessary for safety review, abuse prevention, investigation handling, dispute resolution, or legal compliance.

## 12. Your Choices
You may be able to:
- update or delete profile fields
- replace or remove uploaded media
- close or stop using your account

Keep in mind:
- public content may remain visible until changes propagate
- cached copies, logs, backups, and third-party delivery layers may persist for some time
- some records may be retained for safety, legal, operational, or abuse-prevention reasons

## 13. Security
- We use reasonable administrative, technical, and operational measures intended to help protect information.
- No service can guarantee absolute security.
- We do not promise that Overclock will always be secure or that unauthorized access will never occur.
- You are responsible for maintaining the security of your Discord account and your own devices.

## 14. International Storage and Processing
- Your information may be stored or processed in countries other than where you live, depending on our providers and infrastructure.
- By using Overclock, you understand that data may be transferred to and processed in jurisdictions with different data protection rules.

## 15. Children's Privacy
- Overclock is not intended for children under 13.
- If we learn that we have knowingly collected personal information from a child under the minimum permitted age, we may delete that information and restrict the related account.

## 16. Future Features

- We may use information in ways reasonably related to those future features, consistent with this Privacy Policy.
- If our practices materially change, we may update this Privacy Policy and revise the date above.

## 17. Changes to This Policy

- If we make material changes, we may provide additional notice where appropriate.
- Your continued use of Overclock after an update means you accept the revised Privacy Policy.
