# Brand Guidelines

## Scope
- Based on current shipped identity only
- Sources:
  - `overclock/app/components/global-auth-bar.tsx`
  - `overclock/app/components/global-footer.tsx`
  - `overclock/app/layout.tsx`
  - `overclock/public/branding/*`
  - `overclock/public/oc-logos/*`

## Brand Core
- Brand name is presented as `overclock.lol`
- Tone is lower-case, direct, and internet-native
- Brand atmosphere is:
  - premium dark UI
  - competitive
  - calm
  - focused
  - slightly underground

## Emotional Goals
- Feel like a serious player space, not a novelty app
- Feel immersive without becoming theatrical
- Feel premium through restraint
- Feel trustworthy enough for profile identity and connections

## Brand Adjectives
- competitive
- sleek
- muted
- compact
- polished
- nocturnal
- modern
- intentional

## What The Brand Should Feel Like
- A dark competitive lobby
- A high-signal player profile system
- A refined gaming utility
- A product made for players who care about role/rank fit

## What The Brand Should Never Feel Like
- Mascot-heavy esports merch
- RGB overload
- colorful streamer dashboard
- generic productivity SaaS
- cartoonish gamer UI
- bloated social network

## Tone
- concise
- confident
- practical
- player-aware
- not corporate
- not ironic or chaotic in UI copy

## Visual Atmosphere
- Near-black surfaces with faint texture
- Soft white and sky bloom used sparingly
- Stronger imagery reserved for:
  - player covers
  - avatars
  - hero portraits
  - rank icons
- UI chrome stays minimal and dark

## Typography Personality
- Geist Sans keeps the voice contemporary and clean
- Tight tracking gives headings a compact, assertive tone
- Lowercase product naming softens the otherwise premium-dark aesthetic
- Typography should feel engineered, not ornamental

## Iconography Style
- Use simple modern UI icons from `lucide-react`
- Use official-looking brand icons for socials from `react-icons`
- Hero and rank art provide game specificity; general UI icons stay minimal
- Avoid illustrated icon sets, mascots, or neon gaming pictograms

## Color Behavior
- Base palette:
  - black / near-black backgrounds
  - zinc text scale
  - faint white borders
  - sky accent
- Accent colors are functional:
  - sky for focus, presence, primary save state
  - amber for selective warnings or platform-adjacent highlights
  - role/rank art can introduce color, but the UI shell should not compete

## Glow Usage
- Acceptable:
  - soft rank glows
  - restrained sky focus rings
  - subtle radial page bloom
- Not acceptable:
  - neon strokes around all cards
  - rainbow halos
  - permanent animated glow on non-status controls

## Competitive / Gaming Identity
- Comes from:
  - Overwatch hero imagery
  - rank iconography
  - role language
  - profile covers
  - dense LFG scanning
- Should not come from:
  - flashy esports cliches
  - sponsor-style branding
  - oversized hype graphics

## Minimalist Philosophy
- Minimalism here means low clutter, not empty whitespace
- Information can be dense as long as it is:
  - structured
  - muted
  - hierarchy-driven
- Avoid decorative filler that does not improve scan speed or identity

## Premium Dark UI Direction
- Premium comes from:
  - material depth
  - controlled contrast
  - compact typography
  - consistent rounding
  - polished micro-interactions
- Premium does not mean:
  - glassmorphism
  - chrome-heavy futurism
  - luxury gold palettes

## Logo Usage
- Current primary mark in app chrome:
  - `kitty-white-cross-white-border.png`
- It is used small, left-aligned, next to the wordmark
- Current usage points:
  - global header
  - footer
- Rule:
  - keep logo on dark backgrounds
  - pair with `overclock.lol` wordmark in simple text when used in navigation

## Spacing Around Logo
- Current lockup uses tight but breathable spacing:
  - roughly `gap-3`
  - icon around `28px` to `32px`
- Preserve clear space equal to at least the icon border thickness on all sides
- Do not crowd the mark with extra badges, outlines, or taglines

## Favicon / Icon Direction
- `app/favicon.ico` exists and should stay aligned with the current dark-mode brand mark direction
- Small icons should prioritize:
  - silhouette clarity
  - high contrast on dark tabs
  - minimal detail

## Acceptable Imagery
- Overwatch hero portraits
- player avatars
- player-selected cover images
- rank emblems
- subtle textured backgrounds

## Background Treatment
- Preferred:
  - near-black fills
  - masked dot-grid textures
  - subtle radial light bloom
  - cover-image darkening overlays
- Avoid:
  - flat bright backgrounds
  - loud abstract illustrations
  - colorful panel gradients

## Acceptable Motion
- Short fade / lift entrances
- Tiny hover brightening
- Small hero tile scale
- Presence ping animation
- Motion should feel ambient and supportive, not gamified

## Forbidden Aesthetics
- esports mascot branding
- loud gradients
- generic SaaS hero sections
- colorful gaming UI overload
- excessive glassmorphism
- oversized UI
- bloated dashboards
- bright card stacks on gray backgrounds
- playful arcade styling

## Acceptable Inspirations
- dark competitive lobbies
- premium game companion apps
- modern profile-driven communities
- minimalist control panels with game-native artwork

## Do
- Keep branding quiet and dark
- Let player identity and rank art provide the personality
- Use the logo as a clean mark, not a centerpiece illustration
- Preserve the lower-case `overclock.lol` voice

## Do Not
- Do not introduce mascot-first branding
- Do not push multi-color gradient branding into app chrome
- Do not make the product feel like a generic startup dashboard
- Do not increase UI size just to feel more "premium"
